Privacy in the Lord of the Rings Universe

Exploring the Ethics and Surveillance Power of Middle-earth Artifacts

The One Ring

The One Ring doesn't just make its wearer invisible — it connects them to the Eye of Sauron. While it grants immense power, it’s also a beacon that compromises privacy and autonomy. This duality raises important ethical questions about tools that offer anonymity but also enable surveillance.

The Palantír

The Palantíri were meant for communication, but became tools of manipulation and surveillance. Once Sauron gained access, even well-meaning users like Saruman fell under his influence. The Palantír echoes modern concerns about information transparency, manipulation, and the trustworthiness of what we see through our devices.

Gollum's Obsession

Though not a magical artifact himself, Gollum is a study in the psychological impact of being surveilled and corrupted. The long-term exposure to the Ring’s power shaped his identity and stripped away his privacy, autonomy, and sense of self.

The Mirror of Galadriel

This enchanted mirror reveals possible futures and hidden truths — but only to those who dare to look. While it’s a powerful foresight tool, it raises ethical questions around consent, unintended exposure, and the burden of knowing too much.
